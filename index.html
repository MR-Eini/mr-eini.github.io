About Me

  • I am Mohammad Reza Eini, a dedicated and innovative PhD student specializing in Environmental Engineering, with a focus on hydrology, meteorology, and water management. I am currently pursuing my doctoral studies at the Warsaw University of Life Sciences (SGGW), where I have been deeply involved in research related to the impacts of climate change on water resources. My academic journey has taken me from a Bachelor's degree in Water Engineering at Tabriz University to a Master's degree in Water Resources Engineering from the University of Tehran, culminating in my current PhD work where I am exploring the complexities of droughts and water management under changing climatic conditions. Throughout my academic and professional career, I have been fortunate to engage in research collaborations with renowned institutions such as the Research Institute for Geo-Hydrological Protection in Italy, the Potsdam Institute for Climate Impact Research in Germany, and the Helmholtz Centre for Environmental Research. These experiences have enriched my understanding of global water resource challenges and enhanced my ability to contribute meaningfully to the field.

Research Interests

  • My research is primarily focused on developing and applying innovative methodologies to assess the impacts of climate change on water resources, particularly in relation to long-term surface and subsurface water storage, flood and drought occurrences, ecosystem health, and agricultural productivity. I am passionate about integrating cutting-edge modeling techniques with advanced observational data to create comprehensive models that can predict and mitigate the adverse environmental effects of climate change.One of my key research areas is the enhancement of hydrological models by incorporating sustainable technologies and local practices. I aim to improve the accuracy and efficiency of these models to better inform water resource management strategies. Additionally, I am deeply interested in exploring the socio-economic implications of water resource management, striving to work collaboratively with stakeholders to design and implement adaptive strategies that ensure water security and sustainability in the face of evolving climatic conditions.My work involves the use of advanced computational tools and techniques, including the Soil and Water Assessment Tool (SWAT), SWAT-MODFLOW, and various data-driven methods in water science such as Artificial Neural Networks (ANN) and Support Vector Machines (SVM). My ultimate goal is to contribute to the development of robust, adaptable, and sustainable water management systems that can withstand the challenges posed by climate change.
